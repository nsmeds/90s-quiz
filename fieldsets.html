Ready to test your knowledge of 1990s indie rock culture? These ten probing questions will determine whether you have what it takes to properly reminisce with the disaffected slackers of yesteryear.

Pavement's original drummer was:

Gary Young played drums in Pavement from 1989-1993 and recorded the band's groundbreaking early work at Louder Than You Think, his home studio in Stockton, CA.

The two principal members of Royal Trux went on to form all the following bands except:

Jennifer Herrema and Neil Hagerty have each formed several new outfits, but to date neither has performed or recorded with British rock band XTC.

Pacific Northwest record label Kill Rock Stars released albums by all of the following except:

Beat Happening was led by Calvin Johnson, founder of K Records - the 'other' Olympia label and the supposed archrival of Kill Rock Stars.

Singer and guitarist Chan Marshall records and performs under the name:

Chan Marshall is, of course, better known as Cat Power, and is shown here demonstrating her own impeccable taste in '90s indie rock.

Dale Crover has played drums for which of the following bands?

Mister Crover is the longtime drummer of the Melvins and played on some of Nirvana's earliest recordings.

Tortoise is from:

Like the Bears and the Bulls, the band Tortoise is a venerable Chicago institution.

The Elephant Six Collective included all of the following bands except:

The Elephant Six tentacles spread far and wide, but Superchunk has never been associated with the Athens crew.

Two members of Blonde Redhead are:

Simone and Amedeo Pace are twin brothers. According to legend, vocalist/guitarist Kazu Makino demanded that one of them grow a mustache so she could tell them apart.

Bill Callahan, aka Smog, wrote a zine by the name of:

Bill's zine was called Disaster, and so was the label on which he self-released his early recordings.

Chris Lombardi is the founder of:

Suave gentleman Chris Lombardi founded Matador Records in New York City in 1989. Now he is rich!
